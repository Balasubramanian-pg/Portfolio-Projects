## Overview of Sprint 9.1: Roadmap Development

All the machinery is built.
Detection works. Sentiment listens. Simulation predicts. Dashboards speak. Governance guards. Appeals listen back.

Sprint 9.1 asks the final strategic question:

* *What does this system become over time?*

A roadmap is not a checklist. It is a **trajectory**. It defines how your system evolves from:

* Functional → Reliable → Intelligent → Autonomous → Strategic

This sprint creates a **multi-phase maturity blueprint** that aligns:

* Technology
* Business value
* Risk control
* Scalability

---

## What a Maturity Roadmap Must Achieve

A strong roadmap:

* Defines clear maturity stages
* Aligns technical evolution with business outcomes
* Prioritizes investments
* Anticipates risks and future needs

It answers:

* Where are we today?
* Where do we want to go?
* What steps get us there?
* What capabilities unlock the next level?

---

## Phase 1: Current State Assessment (Baseline Maturity)

### 1. Evaluate Current System Capabilities

At this point, your system likely includes:

* Deterministic detection rules
* Probabilistic models
* Sentiment and entity analysis
* Simulation engine
* Dashboard and reporting
* Governance and appeals

---

### 2. Define Current Maturity Level

Typical classification:

* **Level 1: Reactive**

  * Basic detection
  * Limited insights

* **Level 2: Structured**

  * Multi-layer detection
  * Reporting dashboards

* **Level 3: Integrated**

  * End-to-end pipeline
  * Governance and feedback loops

ASSUMPTION:

* Your system is at **Level 3 (Integrated)**

---

### 3. Identify Current Gaps

Examples:

* Limited automation
* Partial real-time capabilities
* Manual intervention in moderation
* Limited predictive intelligence
